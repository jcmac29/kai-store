Free AI Prompt Resources

Copy-paste ready prompts and templates for ChatGPT, Claude, and other AI tools. Use these as starting points for your own prompts.

Universal Prompt Framework

Use this structure for any AI prompt:

Role: Act as a [EXPERT TYPE] with expertise in [DOMAIN]. Context: I'm working on [SITUATION/PROJECT]. My goal is [OBJECTIVE]. Task: [SPECIFIC REQUEST] Format: Provide your response as [FORMAT: bullet points/numbered list/paragraphs/table]. Constraints: [ANY LIMITATIONS OR REQUIREMENTS]
Pro Tip: The more specific your context and constraints, the better the AI output. Vague prompts get vague responses.

Free Prompt Templates by Category

Business & Strategy

Act as a business strategist. Analyze my business idea: Business: [DESCRIBE YOUR BUSINESS] Target Market: [WHO YOU SERVE] Current Challenge: [MAIN PROBLEM] Provide: 1. SWOT analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) 2. Three growth strategies ranked by feasibility 3. Key metrics I should track 4. One action I can take this week

Marketing & Copywriting

Act as a direct response copywriter. Write compelling copy for: Product/Service: [WHAT YOU'RE SELLING] Target Audience: [WHO BUYS THIS] Main Benefit: [PRIMARY VALUE] Price Point: [COST] Create: 1. A headline that stops scrolling 2. Three bullet points highlighting benefits (not features) 3. A call-to-action that creates urgency 4. One testimonial-style proof statement

Content Creation

Act as a content strategist. Create a content outline for: Topic: [SUBJECT] Platform: [WHERE IT WILL BE PUBLISHED] Audience: [WHO WILL READ/WATCH] Goal: [WHAT ACTION SHOULD THEY TAKE] Provide: 1. Hook/opening that grabs attention 2. Key points to cover (5-7 points) 3. Examples or stories to include 4. Strong closing with clear CTA

Coding & Development

Act as a senior software engineer. Help me with: Language/Framework: [TECH STACK] Current Code: [PASTE CODE OR DESCRIBE] Problem: [WHAT'S NOT WORKING] Expected Behavior: [WHAT SHOULD HAPPEN] Please: 1. Identify the root cause 2. Provide corrected code 3. Explain why the fix works 4. Suggest any improvements for code quality

Email Writing

Act as an email copywriter. Write a [TYPE: cold/follow-up/sales/newsletter] email: Sender: [WHO I AM] Recipient: [WHO THEY ARE] Relationship: [COLD/WARM/EXISTING CUSTOMER] Goal: [WHAT I WANT THEM TO DO] Key Point: [MAIN MESSAGE] Requirements: - Subject line that gets opened - Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max) - One clear call-to-action - Professional but conversational tone

Learning & Research

Act as an expert teacher in [SUBJECT]. I want to learn [SPECIFIC TOPIC]. My current level: [BEGINNER/INTERMEDIATE/ADVANCED] Learning goal: [WHAT I WANT TO ACHIEVE] Time available: [HOW MUCH TIME I HAVE] Create a learning plan that includes: 1. Key concepts I must understand (prioritized) 2. Common misconceptions to avoid 3. Practical exercises to apply knowledge 4. Resources for deeper learning 5. How to know when I've mastered this

Prompt Engineering Tips

1. Be Specific: "Write marketing copy" → "Write a 100-word product description for eco-friendly water bottles targeting health-conscious millennials"
2. Give Examples: Show the AI what good output looks like. "Format like this: [example]"
3. Assign a Role: "Act as a CFO" gets different output than "Act as a startup founder"
4. Specify Format: Request bullet points, tables, JSON, or whatever structure you need
5. Iterate: First response not perfect? Say "Make it more concise" or "Add more examples"
