Maximize what you get without paying.

- ChatGPT for writing drafts
- Grammarly for editing
- Canva for graphics
- Buffer for scheduling

Together, they form a complete toolkit.

### Strategy 2: Daily Limit Rotation

When you hit daily limits:
- Switch between ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini
- Use different image generators
- Spread usage across tools

### Strategy 3: Focus on What Matters

Free tiers often have everything you need for core tasks. You only need premium when you:
- Hit consistent usage limits
- Need team features
- Require premium integrations
- Want to remove branding

### Strategy 4: Check for Upgrades

Companies frequently expand free tiers. Check back monthly for:
- Increased limits
- New free features
- Promotional offers

- Plan your high-value uses

- Track your usage

## When to Upgrade

Free tools are powerful, but sometimes paid makes sense.

### Signs You Should Upgrade:

1. **Constantly hitting limits** - Time wasted waiting for resets
2. **Need team features** - Collaboration requires paid tiers
3. **Professional deliverables** - Removing watermarks/branding
4. **Workflow efficiency** - Integrations save significant time
5. **Higher quality outputs** - Premium models produce better results

### Best Value Paid Upgrades:

If you can only pay for a few tools:

1. **ChatGPT Plus ($20/month)** - Most versatile upgrade
2. **Canva Pro ($13/month)** - Unlocks massive design library
3. **Grammarly Premium ($12/month)** - Better writing everywhere
