## Plot & Story Structure Prompts (6)

### 1. Story Premise Developer
```
Help me develop my story premise into a complete concept.

Initial idea: [your basic concept]
Genre: [fiction genre]
Target audience: [who reads this]
Length: [novel, novella, short story]
Tone: [dark, humorous, literary, etc.]

Develop this into:
1. High-concept logline (1 sentence)
2. Elevator pitch (2-3 sentences)
3. Expanded premise (1 paragraph)
4. Core conflict (internal and external)
5. Stakes (what's at risk)
6. Theme exploration (deeper meaning)
7. Unique angle (what makes this fresh)
8. Comparable titles (for positioning)
9. Questions the story answers
10. The emotional journey readers will take
```

### 2. Three-Act Structure Planner
```
Help me plot my story using three-act structure.

Premise: [your story concept]
Protagonist: [main character]
Main conflict: [central problem]
Genre: [your genre]

Create detailed outline:

ACT 1 (Setup - 25%):
- Opening scene/hook
- Status quo establishment
- Inciting incident
- First turning point

ACT 2 (Confrontation - 50%):
- Rising action beats
- Midpoint shift
- Subplot integration
- All is lost moment

ACT 3 (Resolution - 25%):
- Dark night of the soul
- Climax buildup
- Climax scene
- Resolution and denouement

For each beat, provide: what happens, why it matters, and how it advances character growth.
```

### 3. Plot Hole Finder
```
Analyze my plot for holes and inconsistencies.

Story summary: [describe your plot]
Main characters: [list them and roles]
Key plot points: [major events]
World rules: [any special rules of your story world]
Timeline: [when things happen]

Analyze for:
1. Logic gaps (things that don't make sense)
2. Character motivation inconsistencies
3. Timeline problems
4. Cause-and-effect breaks
5. Deus ex machina moments
6. Unresolved threads
7. Stakes inconsistencies
8. World-building contradictions
9. Convenience plotting
10. Missing scenes (implied but not shown)

For each issue: what's wrong, why it matters, and how to fix it.
```

### 4. Subplot Weaver
```
Help me develop and integrate subplots into my main story.

Main plot: [your central story]
Theme: [what your story is about]
Main characters: [protagonist and key players]
Current subplots: [if any]
Genre: [your genre]
Length: [word count target]

Create subplot suggestions:
1. Character-driven subplot (internal journey)
2. Relationship subplot (connection between characters)
3. Thematic subplot (reinforces theme)
4. Contrast subplot (shows alternative path)
5. Mystery/reveal subplot (adds intrigue)

For each:
- What it is
- Which characters involved
- How it connects to main plot
- Where it intersects (specific beats)
- How it resolves
- Why it strengthens the story
```

### 5. Scene-by-Scene Outline
```
Create a detailed scene-by-scene outline.

Story summary: [your complete plot]
Structure: [act breakdown if you have one]
POV approach: [single POV, multiple, etc.]
Target word count: [your goal]

For each scene, provide:
1. Scene number and chapter
2. POV character
3. Scene goal (what must happen)
4. Conflict in scene
5. Setting
6. Characters present
7. Opening hook
8. Key beats
9. Scene ending (disaster or resolution)
10. Word count estimate
11. How it connects to next scene

Format as a working document I can use while drafting.
```

### 6. Story Ending Generator
```
Help me brainstorm and choose the right ending.

Story setup: [premise and conflict]
Character arc: [protagonist's journey]
Theme: [what your story explores]
Genre expectations: [what readers expect]
Tone: [how you want readers to feel]

Generate 5 possible endings:
1. The expected ending (genre-appropriate)
2. The twist ending (surprise but earned)
3. The bittersweet ending (win with loss)
4. The tragic ending (character fails)
5. The ambiguous ending (reader decides)

For each:
- What happens
- How it completes character arc
- How it reinforces theme
- Reader emotional impact
- Why it works or doesn't
- Foreshadowing needed

Then recommend which fits best and why.
```

## Character Development Prompts (6)

### 7. Character Profile Builder
```
Help me create a deep, complex character.

Basic concept: [your initial idea for this character]
Role in story: [protagonist, antagonist, supporting]
Genre: [your story's genre]
Setting: [when and where]

Build complete profile:

EXTERNAL:
- Name and meaning
- Age and appearance
- Occupation/role
- Social status
- Speaking style
- Mannerisms and habits

INTERNAL:
- Core desire (what they want)
- Core fear (what they avoid)
- Core wound (past trauma)
- Core belief (worldview)
- Core misbelief (flaw to overcome)

BACKGROUND:
- Childhood defining moment
- Key relationships
- Formative experiences
- Skills and expertise
- Secrets

STORY:
- Character arc trajectory
- Starting state vs. ending state
- Key growth moments
```

### 8. Character Voice Differentiator
```
Help me create distinct voices for my characters.

Characters:
[Character 1]: [brief description]
[Character 2]: [brief description]
[Character 3]: [brief description]
[Add more as needed]

For each character, define:
1. Vocabulary level and style
2. Sentence structure patterns
3. Verbal tics and catchphrases
4. Topics they gravitate toward
5. Topics they avoid
6. How they express emotion
7. Humor style (if any)
8. Speech rhythm
9. Subtext patterns (what they don't say)
10. Sample dialogue (3-5 lines)

Then write a conversation between them showing distinct voices.
```

### 9. Antagonist Developer
```
Create a compelling antagonist for my story.

Story premise: [your story]
Protagonist: [your hero]
Theme: [what you're exploring]
Genre: [your genre]

Build antagonist:
1. Core motivation (why they oppose protagonist)
2. Backstory that justifies their worldview
3. How they see themselves as the hero
4. What they're right about
5. Their methods (how they pursue goals)
6. Strengths that make them formidable
7. Weaknesses that could defeat them
8. Relationship to protagonist
9. What they want vs. what they need
10. Potential for redemption (if any)
11. How they challenge protagonist's beliefs
12. Their own character arc
```

### 10. Character Relationship Mapper
```
Map and develop relationships between my characters.

Characters:
[List your main characters with brief descriptions]

For each key relationship, develop:
1. History (how they met, shared past)
2. Current dynamic (power balance, tension)
3. What each wants from the other
4. What each fears about the relationship
5. Source of conflict between them
6. Source of connection/bond
7. How they challenge each other
8. How relationship changes through story
9. Key scenes that define relationship
10. Subtext in their interactions

Create relationship map showing connections and tensions.
```

### 11. Character Motivation Deep Dive
```
Help me understand my character's motivations deeply.

Character: [who they are]
Their goal in the story: [what they want]
What I know about their past: [background]
Current situation: [where they start]

Analyze and develop:
1. Surface motivation (stated goal)
2. Deeper motivation (real goal)
3. Unconscious motivation (hidden need)
4. What they think will make them happy
5. What would actually make them happy
6. The gap between those (source of arc)
7. Why they want it NOW (urgency)
8. What they're willing to sacrifice
9. Lines they won't cross (until they do)
10. How motivation shifts through story
11. Moment of realization
12. Decision that defines them
```

### 12. Character Arc Designer
```
Design a complete character arc.

Character: [who they are]
Starting state: [who they are at beginning]
Story events: [what happens to them]
Theme: [what their journey represents]
Arc type: [positive change, negative, flat]

Design arc:
1. The Lie they believe (misbelief)
2. The Truth they need to learn
3. The Ghost (past event causing Lie)
4. The Want (conscious goal)
5. The Need (unconscious goal)
6. Inciting incident impact on belief
7. Key turning points (belief challenged)
8. Midpoint shift (glimpse of Truth)
9. Dark moment (return to Lie or final temptation)
10. Climactic choice (demonstrates change)
11. New normal (transformed state)
12. Physical and behavioral changes throughout
```

## Editing & Revision Prompts (6)

### 13. First Draft Diagnostic
```
Analyze my first draft and identify priorities for revision.

I'll paste a chapter/section: [paste your text]

Or describe your draft:
- Word count: [length]
- Main issues I'm sensing: [your concerns]
- What I think works: [strengths]

Provide diagnostic on:
1. Structural issues (pacing, plot)
2. Character consistency
3. Voice and POV
4. Scene construction
5. Dialogue effectiveness
6. Description balance
7. Show vs. tell ratio
8. Tension maintenance
9. Clarity of prose
10. Opening and ending strength

Prioritize: what to fix first, second, third.
```

### 14. Scene Revision Workshop
```
Help me revise this scene.

[Paste your scene]

Context:
- Scene goal: [what must happen]
- POV character: [whose head we're in]
- What comes before: [prior scene]
- What comes after: [next scene]

Analyze:
1. Does the scene open with a hook?
2. Is the goal clear?
3. Is there sufficient conflict?
4. Are the stakes present?
5. Is the POV consistent and deep?
6. Does dialogue serve the scene?
7. Is there sensory detail?
8. Does the scene have momentum?
9. Does it end at the right moment?
10. Is every word earning its place?

Then provide specific revision suggestions with examples.
```

### 15. Dialogue Polish
```
Help me improve this dialogue.

[Paste dialogue section]

Context:
- Who is speaking: [characters]
- What's happening: [scene context]
- Subtext goal: [what's not being said]
- Character dynamics: [relationship]

Evaluate and revise:
1. Does each character have distinct voice?
2. Is the dialogue doing more than one job?
3. Are there information dumps disguised as dialogue?
4. Is there enough conflict/tension?
5. Does subtext exist beneath surface?
6. Are dialogue tags invisible?
7. Is there appropriate physical action?
8. Does it sound natural when read aloud?
9. Can any lines be cut?
10. Does emotion come through?

Provide revised version with explanations for changes.
```

### 16. Prose Tightening
```
Help me tighten and strengthen my prose.

[Paste paragraph or page]

Check for and revise:
1. Redundant words and phrases
2. Passive voice (when unnecessary)
3. Filter words (felt, saw, heard, etc.)
4. Weak verbs (replace with stronger ones)
5. Adverb overuse
6. Abstract language (make concrete)
7. Cliches and dead metaphors
8. Unnecessary qualifiers
9. Awkward rhythms
10. Unnecessary "to be" constructions

Provide tightened version and explain key changes.
Show before/after for key improvements.
```

### 17. Pacing Analyzer
```
Analyze the pacing of my manuscript or section.

[Describe or paste your content]

Word count: [length]
Genre: [your genre]
Key scenes/beats: [major moments]

Analyze:
1. Scene length variation
2. Action vs. reflection balance
3. Tension curve
4. Sequel-scene balance
5. Chapter ending hooks
6. Sagging middle diagnosis
7. Rushed vs. drawn-out sections
8. Time skips (too many, too few?)
9. Summary vs. scene balance
10. Build to climax

Provide:
- Visual pacing map
- Specific slow spots to cut or accelerate
- Specific rushed spots to expand
- Structural suggestions
```

### 18. Show Don't Tell Converter
```
Help me convert telling to showing.

[Paste section with telling]

Or provide examples of your telling sentences.

For each instance:
1. Identify the telling
2. Explain why it's weak
3. Provide showing alternative
4. Explain what makes showing work

Also teach me:
- When telling is actually okay
- Quick tests to identify telling
- Techniques for showing emotion
- Techniques for showing character
- Techniques for showing setting
- The telling/showing balance for my genre
```
