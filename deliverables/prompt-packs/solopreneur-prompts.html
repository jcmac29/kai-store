Solopreneur Prompts

35 Battle-Tested AI Prompts to Scale Your One-Person Business

Systems & Automation (8 Prompts)

1. SOP Generator

I need to create a Standard Operating Procedure for [TASK/PROCESS]. Context: - This task is performed [frequency: daily/weekly/monthly] - Current time to complete: [X minutes/hours] - Tools involved: [list tools] - Who performs this: [role] Create a detailed SOP that includes: 1. Purpose and scope 2. Prerequisites and tools needed 3. Step-by-step instructions (numbered) 4. Quality checkpoints 5. Common mistakes to avoid 6. Troubleshooting section 7. Time estimates per step Format for easy reference and training new team members.
Pro tip: Run this for your top 5 time-consuming tasks. You'll have a delegation-ready playbook.

2. Process Audit

Analyze my current workflow for [BUSINESS AREA]: Current process: [Describe your current steps] Time spent: [X hours/week] Pain points: [List frustrations] Tools used: [List current tools] Identify: 1. Redundant steps that can be eliminated 2. Tasks that can be automated (and suggest tools) 3. Tasks that can be batched together 4. Bottlenecks causing delays 5. Quick wins (changes taking <1 hour to implement) 6. Estimated time savings for each improvement Prioritize recommendations by impact vs effort.

3. Automation Opportunity Finder

I run a [TYPE] business as a solopreneur. My weekly tasks include: [List your recurring tasks] For each task, evaluate: 1. Can this be fully automated? (Yes/Partial/No) 2. Recommended automation tool 3. Setup time required 4. Ongoing maintenance needed 5. Cost (free/paid) 6. Hours saved per week Focus on tools that integrate well together. I currently use: [your current stack]

4. Email Template Library Creator

Create a library of email templates for my [BUSINESS TYPE] covering: 1. Initial inquiry response 2. Quote/proposal follow-up 3. Project kickoff 4. Progress update 5. Requesting feedback 6. Invoice reminder (friendly) 7. Invoice reminder (firm) 8. Project completion 9. Referral request 10. Re-engagement (past client) For each template: - Subject line - Body (keep under 150 words) - [BRACKETS] for personalization - Professional but warm tone Make them sound human, not corporate.

5. Decision Matrix Builder

I need to decide between [OPTION A] vs [OPTION B] for my business. Context: [Brief situation description] Create a weighted decision matrix considering: - Cost (initial and ongoing) - Time investment - Revenue potential - Risk level - Scalability - Alignment with long-term goals - Opportunity cost Weight each factor by importance to a solopreneur. Score each option 1-10. Provide a clear recommendation with reasoning.

6. Weekly Review Template

Create a weekly review framework for a solopreneur that takes 30 minutes max. Include sections for: 1. Wins (what went well) 2. Lessons (what I learned) 3. Metrics review (key numbers) 4. Client/project status 5. Next week's top 3 priorities 6. One thing to stop doing 7. One thing to start doing 8. Energy/motivation check Format as a fillable template with prompting questions for each section.

7. Tool Stack Optimizer

Audit my current tool stack for redundancies and gaps: Current tools: [List all your subscriptions and tools] Monthly cost: [Total] Analyze: 1. Overlapping features I'm paying for twice 2. Underutilized tools (paying but not using) 3. Missing tools that would save significant time 4. Better alternatives at lower cost 5. Integration opportunities I'm missing Recommend an optimized stack with estimated monthly savings.

8. Client Onboarding System

Design a client onboarding system for my [SERVICE TYPE] business. Current pain points: - [List issues: missed info, back-and-forth, etc.] Create: 1. Welcome email sequence (3 emails) 2. Information gathering questionnaire (key questions only) 3. Kickoff call agenda (30 min max) 4. Client expectations document 5. Project timeline template 6. Communication guidelines Goal: Professional first impression while minimizing my time investment.

Growth & Marketing (9 Prompts)

9. Ideal Client Profile Builder

Help me define my ideal client for [MY SERVICE/PRODUCT]. Current clients I love working with: [Describe 2-3 best clients] Current clients that drain me: [Describe problem client traits] Build a detailed ideal client profile: 1. Demographics (industry, size, role) 2. Psychographics (values, priorities) 3. Pain points they experience 4. Goals they're trying to achieve 5. Where they spend time online 6. What triggers them to seek help 7. Budget range 8. Red flags to watch for Format as a one-page reference document.

10. Content Pillar Strategy

I'm a [YOUR ROLE] who helps [TARGET AUDIENCE] with [TRANSFORMATION]. Create 5 content pillars for my business: For each pillar: 1. Core topic/theme 2. Why my audience cares 3. 10 subtopic ideas 4. Content formats that work best 5. How it leads to my paid offer Ensure pillars cover: education, inspiration, behind-the-scenes, social proof, and direct promotion. Focus on content I can create in batches efficiently.

11. Lead Magnet Creator

Create a high-converting lead magnet concept for my [BUSINESS TYPE]. My target audience struggles with: [MAIN PAIN POINT] My paid offer is: [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE] Generate 5 lead magnet ideas that: 1. Solve one specific, immediate problem 2. Can be consumed in under 15 minutes 3. Naturally lead to my paid offer 4. Are easy for me to create (no video) 5. Have a compelling title For the best option, outline the full content structure.

12. Referral System Designer

Design a referral system for my [SERVICE] business. Current situation: - Average client value: $[X] - Current referral rate: [X]% - Best referral sources: [describe] Create: 1. Referral ask script (natural, not pushy) 2. Referral incentive structure 3. Follow-up sequence when referred 4. Thank you process for referrers 5. Tracking system (simple) Goal: Systematize referrals without feeling salesy.

13. LinkedIn Content Calendar

Create a 30-day LinkedIn content calendar for a [YOUR ROLE]. My expertise: [TOPICS] My offer: [WHAT YOU SELL] My voice: [Professional/Casual/Bold/etc.] For each day, provide: 1. Content type (story, tip, question, etc.) 2. Hook (first line) 3. Key points to cover 4. Call to action Mix: 40% value, 30% stories, 20% engagement, 10% promotion. Include 5 posts I can repurpose from client wins.

14. Cold Outreach Sequence

Write a 3-email cold outreach sequence for reaching [TARGET PROSPECT]. My service: [WHAT YOU OFFER] Value I provide: [OUTCOMES/RESULTS] Proof: [BRIEF CASE STUDY OR CREDENTIAL] Requirements: - Email 1: Pattern interrupt + curiosity - Email 2: Value-add + social proof - Email 3: Direct ask + easy next step Keep each email under 100 words. No corporate jargon. Focus on their problem, not my service.

15. Testimonial Request System

Create a testimonial collection system for my [SERVICE]. Include: 1. When to ask (timing in client journey) 2. How to ask (email script) 3. Specific questions that get great quotes 4. Follow-up if they don't respond 5. Permission language for using testimonials 6. Template for turning testimonials into case studies Goal: Get testimonials that address specific objections prospects have.

16. Pricing Page Copy

Write pricing page copy for my [SERVICE/PRODUCT]. What I offer: [DESCRIPTION] Price: $[AMOUNT] Who it's for: [TARGET CUSTOMER] Main result: [OUTCOME] Include: 1. Headline that focuses on transformation 2. 3 pricing tiers (if applicable) with names 3. Feature list (benefit-focused, not feature-focused) 4. FAQ section (5 common objections as questions) 5. Guarantee language 6. Urgency element (ethical) 7. CTA button text options

17. Partnership Pitch Template

Write a partnership pitch for approaching [TYPE OF PARTNER]. My business: [WHAT YOU DO] Their business: [WHAT THEY DO] Synergy: [WHY IT MAKES SENSE] Create a pitch that: 1. Opens with genuine compliment/observation 2. Explains the mutual benefit clearly 3. Proposes a specific, low-risk first collaboration 4. Makes it easy to say yes 5. Includes a clear next step Keep under 200 words. Sound like a peer, not a fan.

Productivity & Focus (8 Prompts)

18. Weekly Planning Session

Help me plan my week as a solopreneur. This week I need to: [List everything on your plate] My energy patterns: - Best focus time: [morning/afternoon/evening] - Low energy time: [when] - Meetings already scheduled: [list] Create a time-blocked schedule that: 1. Protects deep work time 2. Batches similar tasks 3. Includes buffer time 4. Has one "CEO hour" for strategic thinking 5. Leaves Friday afternoon light Identify which tasks to delegate, defer, or delete.

19. Energy Audit

Analyze my task list for energy management: Tasks I do regularly: [List your recurring tasks] For each task, categorize: 1. Energy required (high/medium/low) 2. Energy given back (draining/neutral/energizing) 3. Best time of day to do it 4. Can it be batched with similar tasks? 5. Should I be doing this at all? Create an "ideal week" template that respects my energy, not just my calendar.

20. Distraction Elimination Plan

I'm struggling with distractions and context switching. My biggest distractions: [List them honestly] My work environment: [Home office/coworking/etc.] Work hours: [Your schedule] Create a personalized distraction elimination plan: 1. Environment changes 2. Technology boundaries 3. Communication rules to set with clients 4. Time blocking strategy 5. "Distraction ritual" for when I catch myself 6. Accountability mechanism Be specific and actionable. Assume I have limited willpower.

21. Project Scope Definer

Help me scope this project properly: Project: [DESCRIPTION] Client: [TYPE OF CLIENT] My initial quote/timeline: [WHAT YOU SAID] Define: 1. Exactly what's included (be specific) 2. Exactly what's NOT included 3. Assumptions we're making 4. Client responsibilities 5. Revision limits 6. Timeline with milestones 7. Scope change process Format as a client-facing scope document.

22. Meeting Eliminator

Audit my current meeting schedule: Recurring meetings: [List all regular meetings] For each meeting, analyze: 1. Is this meeting necessary? 2. Could it be an async update instead? 3. Could it be shorter? 4. Am I the right person to attend? 5. What's the cost of this meeting (hourly rate x time)? Provide scripts for: - Declining meetings professionally - Converting meetings to async - Shortening meeting requests

23. Batch Content Day Planner

Plan a content batching day for me. Content I need to create: - [X] social posts per week - [X] emails per week - [X] blog posts per month Time available: [X hours] Create a minute-by-minute schedule: 1. Prep/research phase 2. Writing phase 3. Editing phase 4. Scheduling phase Include breaks and buffer time. List everything I need prepared before I start.

24. Decision Fatigue Reducer

Identify decisions I make repeatedly that should be systematized: Areas of my business: [List: client work, marketing, admin, etc.] For each area, identify: 1. Recurring decisions I make 2. The "right" answer 80% of the time 3. A rule or default to eliminate the decision 4. When to override the rule Goal: Reduce daily decisions by creating personal policies.

25. Annual Planning Framework

Help me create my annual plan for [YEAR]. Last year: - Revenue: $[X] - Wins: [list] - Challenges: [list] Next year goals: - Revenue target: $[X] - Other goals: [list] Create: 1. Quarterly revenue targets (with logic) 2. Key initiatives per quarter 3. Skills I need to develop 4. Things I should stop doing 5. Things I should start doing 6. Key metrics to track monthly 7. Quarterly review checkpoints

Client Management (5 Prompts)

26. Scope Creep Response

A client is asking for [ADDITIONAL WORK] that wasn't in the original scope. Original agreement: [WHAT WAS AGREED] What they're asking: [NEW REQUEST] My relationship with this client: [GOOD/ROCKY/NEW] Write a response that: 1. Acknowledges their request positively 2. Clarifies it's outside original scope 3. Offers options (add cost, swap priorities, phase 2) 4. Maintains the relationship 5. Sets a boundary professionally Keep it warm but firm. Under 150 words.

27. Difficult Conversation Script

I need to have a difficult conversation with a client about [ISSUE]. Situation: [DESCRIBE WHAT HAPPENED] My goal: [WHAT OUTCOME I WANT] Their likely reaction: [HOW THEY MIGHT RESPOND] Create a conversation script that: 1. Opens without accusation 2. States facts objectively 3. Expresses impact 4. Proposes a solution 5. Invites their perspective 6. Reaches agreement Include responses for likely objections.

28. Client Feedback Processor

Help me process this client feedback: "[PASTE FEEDBACK]" Analyze: 1. What's the core issue? 2. Is this valid feedback or preference? 3. What's my emotional reaction vs rational assessment? 4. What can I learn from this? 5. What action should I take? 6. How do I prevent this in the future? 7. Draft a professional response

29. Firing a Client Script

I need to end my relationship with a client. Reason: [WHY YOU'RE ENDING IT] Contract status: [TERMS] Outstanding work: [WHAT'S LEFT] Write a professional "breakup" email that: 1. Is clear and decisive 2. Takes responsibility (even if it's not my fault) 3. Offers a transition plan 4. Handles any outstanding work 5. Ends on a professional note 6. Doesn't burn the bridge Keep it brief. No over-explaining.

30. Rate Increase Communication

I'm raising my rates from $[OLD] to $[NEW] for [SERVICE]. Current clients affected: [NUMBER] Relationship length: [VARIES] Market rate: $[RANGE] Value I provide: [OUTCOMES] Write a rate increase announcement that: 1. Gives adequate notice 2. Frames the increase positively 3. Reminds them of value received 4. Offers a loyalty consideration (if appropriate) 5. Makes it easy to continue Create versions for: - Long-term valued clients - Newer clients - Price-sensitive clients

Strategy & Growth (5 Prompts)

31. Revenue Stream Analysis

Analyze my current revenue streams: [List each service/product with revenue] For each stream, evaluate: 1. Revenue (monthly) 2. Profit margin 3. Time investment 4. Scalability (1-10) 5. Enjoyment (1-10) 6. Growth potential 7. Risk if it disappeared Recommend: - What to double down on - What to phase out - New streams to consider based on my skills

32. Productized Service Designer

Help me turn my service into a productized offering. Current service: [WHAT YOU DO] Typical project: [DESCRIBE] Current pricing: [HOW YOU CHARGE] Delivery time: [HOW LONG] Design a productized version: 1. Fixed scope (what's always included) 2. Fixed price 3. Fixed timeline 4. Standard process (repeatable) 5. Clear deliverables 6. FAQ for common questions 7. Ideal client criteria Goal: Reduce customization while increasing perceived value.

33. Competitor Differentiation

Analyze my positioning vs competitors. Me: [YOUR SERVICE/OFFER] Competitor 1: [NAME + WHAT THEY DO] Competitor 2: [NAME + WHAT THEY DO] Competitor 3: [NAME + WHAT THEY DO] Identify: 1. What I do that they don't 2. What they do that I don't 3. Where I'm genuinely better 4. Where they're genuinely better 5. Underserved niches 6. My unique positioning statement Help me find a "category of one" angle.

34. Exit Strategy Planner

Help me think through exit options for my solopreneur business. Business: [WHAT YOU DO] Revenue: $[ANNUAL] Years in business: [X] Key asset: [WHAT MAKES IT VALUABLE] Explore options: 1. Sell the business - what would make it sellable? 2. Transition to passive - what would need to change? 3. Hire replacement - what role would I need? 4. Shut down gracefully - how to do it right? What should I be building now for future options?

35. Monthly Business Review

Conduct a monthly business review for [MONTH]. Metrics: - Revenue: $[X] (goal was $[Y]) - New clients: [X] - Leads generated: [X] - Hours worked: [X] - Effective hourly rate: $[X] What happened: [Brief summary of the month] Analyze: 1. What drove revenue this month? 2. What underperformed and why? 3. Lessons learned 4. What to repeat next month 5. What to stop doing 6. Next month's focus Be honest and actionable.
