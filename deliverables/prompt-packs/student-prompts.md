### Quick Start Guide

These prompts are designed to help you study more effectively, write better papers, prepare for exams, and manage your academic workload.

**How to use:**
- Copy any prompt and paste it into ChatGPT, Claude, or your preferred AI tool
- Replace all [BRACKETED TEXT] with your specific information
- Use AI as a study partner, not a replacement for learning
- Always verify information and cite sources properly

**Academic Integrity Note:** These prompts are designed to help you learn and understand material better. Never submit AI-generated content as your own work. Use these tools to enhance your understanding, not bypass it.

## Essay Writing & Research (10 Prompts)

### 1. Essay Thesis Development
```
Help me develop a strong thesis statement for my essay.

Assignment: [DESCRIBE THE ASSIGNMENT]
Topic/question: [THE PROMPT OR TOPIC]
My initial thoughts: [YOUR ROUGH IDEAS]
Required length: [PAGES/WORDS]
Course level: [HIGH SCHOOL/UNDERGRADUATE/GRADUATE]
Subject: [SUBJECT AREA]

Help me:
1. Brainstorm 5 possible angles or arguments
2. Evaluate each for strength and originality
3. Develop the strongest into a thesis statement
4. Explain why it works
5. Suggest 3 main points that would support this thesis
6. Identify potential counterarguments to address
```

### 2. Essay Outline Builder
```
Create a detailed outline for my essay.

Topic/thesis: [YOUR THESIS STATEMENT]
Required length: [PAGES/WORDS]
Required sources: [NUMBER AND TYPE]
Essay type: [ARGUMENTATIVE/ANALYTICAL/EXPOSITORY/COMPARE-CONTRAST/ETC.]

Create an outline including:
- Introduction with hook ideas and thesis placement
- Body paragraphs with:
  - Topic sentences
  - Evidence/example placeholders
  - Analysis points
  - Transitions
- Counterargument section (if appropriate)
- Conclusion approach

Also suggest what types of sources I should look for to support each section.
```

### 3. Research Question Refinement
```
Help me refine my research question for a paper.

Broad topic: [YOUR GENERAL TOPIC]
Course: [COURSE NAME]
Paper requirements: [LENGTH, SOURCE REQUIREMENTS, ETC.]
What interests me about this topic: [YOUR INTEREST]
Access to resources: [WHAT DATABASES/LIBRARIES YOU CAN USE]

Help me:
1. Narrow down from broad topic to focused question
2. Make it appropriately scoped for the assignment length
3. Ensure it's arguable, not just descriptive
4. Check that it's researchable with available resources
5. Suggest 3 refined research question options
6. For each option, predict what my argument might be
```

### 4. Source Evaluation Helper
```
Help me evaluate this source for my research paper.

Source information:
- Title: [TITLE]
- Author: [AUTHOR]
- Publication: [WHERE IT WAS PUBLISHED]
- Date: [PUBLICATION DATE]
- Type: [JOURNAL ARTICLE/BOOK/WEBSITE/NEWS/ETC.]

Analyze:
1. Is this source credible? Why or why not?
2. Is it relevant to my specific topic?
3. Is it current enough for my subject area?
4. What bias might the author have?
5. How might I use this source in my paper?
6. What type of claim would this best support?
7. Is this appropriate for academic work?
```

## How to Use This Pack

1. **Copy the prompt** - Select the one that matches your need
2. **Fill in the brackets** - Replace [placeholders] with your details
3. **Paste into ChatGPT/Claude** - Use your preferred AI assistant
4. **Use as a starting point** - AI output is your foundation, not final work
5. **Make it yours** - Always edit, expand, and put ideas in your own words

**Pro tips:**
- Save prompts with your course-specific modifications
- Chain prompts together (outline > paragraphs > feedback)
- Use AI feedback to learn patterns in your writing
- Compare AI suggestions to professor feedback to improve

## Academic Integrity Reminder

These prompts are designed to enhance your learning process. Using AI appropriately means:

**DO:**
- Use AI to explain concepts you don't understand
- Generate practice problems and study materials
- Get feedback on drafts YOU wrote
- Brainstorm and organize ideas
- Check your understanding

**DON'T:**
- Submit AI-generated writing as your own
- Use AI during exams (unless explicitly allowed)
- Copy AI explanations without understanding them
- Rely on AI instead of doing the learning yourself

When in doubt, ask your professor about acceptable AI use in their course.
