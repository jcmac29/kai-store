## Essay Writing Prompts (7)

### 1. Essay Outline Generator
```
Create a detailed essay outline for my assignment.

Essay topic: [your topic or prompt]
Essay type: [argumentative, persuasive, analytical, expository, compare/contrast, narrative]
Required length: [word count or page count]
Key requirements: [any specific instructions from professor]
Sources required: [how many, what types]
Thesis idea (if you have one): [your main argument, or "help me develop one"]

Generate:
1. Strong thesis statement (with 2 alternatives)
2. Introduction strategy (hook, context, thesis)
3. Body paragraph breakdown:
   - Topic sentence for each paragraph
   - Main evidence/points to include
   - How each supports the thesis
   - Transitions between sections
4. Conclusion approach
5. Estimated word distribution per section
6. Potential counterarguments to address
```
**What it does:** Creates a comprehensive essay structure so you know exactly what to write before you start.

## Studying & Exam Prep Prompts (7)

### 8. Concept Explainer (Feynman Technique)
```
Explain this concept to me like I'm a beginner, then build up complexity.

Concept: [topic or concept you're studying]
Subject: [course/field]
What I already know: [your current understanding]
What confuses me: [specific parts you don't get]

Provide:
1. Simple analogy explanation (like I'm 12)
2. More detailed explanation (like I'm a college freshman)
3. Full complexity explanation (what I need to know for exams)
4. Common misconceptions about this concept
5. How it connects to related concepts
6. Practice questions to test my understanding
7. One-sentence summary I can use for quick recall
```
**What it does:** Builds understanding from simple to complex, using the Feynman technique for deep learning.

- Type: [journal article, book, website, etc.]

- URL or DOI: [if applicable]

4. Currency (relevance of publication date)

6. Academic acceptability (can you cite this?)

**What it does:** Systematically evaluates sources so you know which ones are credible and how to use them.

## How to Use This Pack

1. **Copy the prompt** - Select the prompt that matches your need
2. **Fill in the brackets** - Replace [placeholders] with your specific details
3. **Paste into ChatGPT/Claude** - Use your preferred AI assistant
4. **Use as a starting point** - AI output should be your foundation, not your final work
5. **Make it yours** - Always edit, expand, and put ideas in your own words

**Important notes on academic integrity:**
- Use AI to LEARN and IMPROVE, not to replace your thinking
- Always understand what you submit - you should be able to explain any part
- Check your school's AI use policies
- Cite AI assistance if your professor requires it
- The goal is to become a better writer/student, not to shortcut learning

**Pro tips:**
- Save your best prompts with your specific modifications
- Chain prompts together (outline -> paragraphs -> feedback)
- Use AI feedback to learn patterns in your writing
- Compare AI suggestions to professor feedback to improve
